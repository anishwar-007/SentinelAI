Rohit Sharma as a white-ball opener

Rohit Sharma is widely regarded as one of the greatest ODI openers of all time. As a specialist opener in ODIs he has recorded three double centuries, the only player to do so. His ODI opening record is often summarized around a mid-50s batting average, a strike rate near 90, and dozens of centuries across a long career at the top of the order.

In T20 cricket Rohit typically plays as an aggressive powerplay opener. He holds major T20I volume records for runs and centuries, usually batting with a strike rate near 140. Teams use him to maximize early overs and put bowling attacks under immediate pressure.

In Test cricket Rohit became a specialist opener from 2019 onward. His home record in India as an opener is especially strong, with a career opening average often cited near the mid-40s overall and higher in home conditions. Away from Asia his average as an opener is materially lower, particularly in SENA countries.

Virat Kohli as an opener versus middle order

Virat Kohli’s primary greatness is at number three in ODIs and number four in Tests, not as a long-term specialist opener. He has opened in ODIs mainly in shorter phases of his career, with elite but smaller-sample opener numbers compared with Rohit’s decade-plus body of work.

In T20 cricket Kohli has increasingly opened, including IPL seasons and the 2024 T20 World Cup. In that phase his T20 opening form has often been summarized around averages above 50 and strike rates near 150, with a style focused on strike rotation, chase control, and holding an innings together when early wickets fall.

Comparing opener roles

If a team wants maximum powerplay impact and long white-ball opener longevity, Rohit Sharma is usually the stronger specialist opener profile. If a team wants high-control chasing and technical consistency under pressure, Kohli’s recent T20 opening form is outstanding, but his historical peak value remains as a middle-order batter rather than a career opener.

A common elite pairing discussed by analysts is Rohit as the high-ceiling striker and Kohli as the high-control partner. That combination appeared in India’s 2024 T20 World Cup campaign and is often used as the reference for modern opening balance.
